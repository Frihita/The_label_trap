THE LABEL TRAP

"How much spinach do you think is actually in a pack of 'Spinach Noodles'?"

"Less than 1.5 percent. Most of the color comes from synthetic or dehydrated extract, not real spinach."

"Marketing uses the green label trick—tiny ingredient, big claim."

"Do you think your favorite tomato ketchup is packed with real tomatoes?"

"Barely. Many commercial ketchups use under 25 percent tomato paste plus sugar, starch, and preservatives."

"That thick texture? It's often corn syrup, not tomato pulp."

"If real fruit juice needs double the fruit to make one glass, how are '100% Real Juices' sold cheaper than fruit?"

"They're mostly reconstituted from fruit concentrate, sugar, and flavoring—not fresh fruit."

"Concentrates travel cheap. Real fruit doesn't."
